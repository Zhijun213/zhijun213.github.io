About

I am a Ph.D student in Computational Media and Arts at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou), supervised by Prof. Kang ZHANG.

Prior to this, I obtained B.Eng degree in Landscape Architecture at Southeast University and M.Phil degree in Computational Media and Arts at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou).

My current research explores the intersection of cultural heritage, human-computer interaction, and computational aesthetics. I focus on the digital computation, generative preservation, and quantitative analysis of intangible cultural heritage, particularly traditional wood carving and clay sculpture in South China.
